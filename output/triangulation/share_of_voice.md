# Share of Voice — Shelf vs. Social, per HK Brand

Share of shelf is % of 950 HK products. Share of voice is % of 1030 brand-attributed XHS posts — 601 posts XHS could not attribute to a tracked brand ("other") are excluded from that denominator, not counted for any brand.

## Reading the gap
- Olens is overindexed on social vs. shelf by 19.2pp (27.1% of voice vs 7.9% of shelf). Based on a smaller base (75 HK products, under 100) — this ratio is more volatile.
- CooperVision is overindexed on social vs. shelf by 7.6pp (16.7% of voice vs 9.1% of shelf). Based on a smaller base (86 HK products, under 100) — this ratio is more volatile.
- Alcon is overindexed on social vs. shelf by 1.3pp (15.1% of voice vs 13.9% of shelf).
- Bausch & Lomb is underindexed on social vs. shelf by 11.6pp (14.8% of voice vs 26.3% of shelf).
- Acuvue is underindexed on social vs. shelf by 16.5pp (26.3% of voice vs 42.8% of shelf).

_Positive gap = overindexed on social relative to shelf (above the y=x reference line on the quadrant chart); negative gap = underindexed (below the line)._

## Volume vs. sentiment — is high voice backed by positive sentiment?
Net sentiment is (% positive − % negative) as a share of THAT BRAND's own XHS posts — a volume-independent quality score, not a share of the grand total. This tests whether high-voice brands are winning on volume alone or on volume AND sentiment.

| Olens | 279 | 71.7% | 11.5% | 16.8% | +60.2% |

### Reading volume against sentiment
- Olens's XHS posts run net positive (+60.2%: 72% positive, 11% negative, 17% neutral). High social voice here is backed by genuinely positive sentiment, not just volume.
- CooperVision's XHS posts run net positive (+27.3%: 47% positive, 19% negative, 34% neutral). Based on a smaller base (172 XHS posts, under 200) — lower-confidence. High social voice here is backed by genuinely positive sentiment, not just volume.
- Alcon's XHS posts run net positive (+41.0%: 61% positive, 20% negative, 19% neutral). Based on a smaller base (156 XHS posts, under 200) — lower-confidence. High social voice here is backed by genuinely positive sentiment, not just volume.
- Bausch & Lomb's XHS posts run net positive (+33.6%: 51% positive, 18% negative, 31% neutral). Based on a smaller base (152 XHS posts, under 200) — lower-confidence.
- Acuvue's XHS posts run net positive (+59.8%: 68% positive, 8% negative, 24% neutral).
